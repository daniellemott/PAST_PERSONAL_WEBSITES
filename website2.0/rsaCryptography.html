RSA Cryptography

RSA cryptography is a public-key cryptosystem based on modular arithmetic. It's named after its creators: Rivest, Shamir, and Adleman. This method of encryption is based on the idea that determining the prime factorization of a large number is almost impossible.

The Premise

Alice wants to send a secret message to Bob. The problem is that Eve is listening to everything that Alice and Bob say to each other. In order to prevent Eve from understanding Alice's message, Alice will send an encrypted message to Bob using the encryption information that Bob communicates to Alice.

How It Works

Bob's Role

  • Bob chooses two prime numbers, P and Q.
  • He computes N, where N = P * Q.
  • He computes Phi(N), where Phi(N) = (P - 1) * (Q - 1).
  • He finds E, where E is relatively prime with Phi(N) and has a modular inverse, D, in mod N.
  • He publishes E and N to Alice (Eve hears this information as well).

Alice's Role

  • Alice chooses the secret integer message, X, she would like to share with Bob.
  • Alice encrypts her message by computing (X ^ E) % N.
  • She publishes her encrypted message to Bob (and subsequently Eve).

Bob

  • Bob decrypts Alice's message, Z, by calculating (Z ^ D) % N.
